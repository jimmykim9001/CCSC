About

Learn about what we've done in the past.

  • August 2015

    Inception

    In the summer of 2015, Brian Lee, Boris Yue and Jimmy Kim all decided to start a competition in the Bay Area. We were driven by our passion for algorithmic competitions, which we had discovered with ACSL, USACO and ProCo. We started to come up with the plans to make the club a reality at Amador Valley High School.

  • December 2015

    First Competition

    As we began to legitimize the club at Amador Valley High School, we contacted high school students from Doughtery Valley High School to Foothill High School to see if anyone was interested. After some research, we found that there was a lot of interest in the Bay Area, interest that was not being satisfied by other sources. So, we held our first competition at Amador Valley High School, where 41 students showed up to compete.

  • April 2016

    Second Competition

    Propelled by the success of the first competition, CCSC is planning its second competition on April 2016. If interested, click here to buy your ticket.

How do I compete?

For our competitions, we will be using hackerrank. This allows us to host online competitions and post the leaderboard. At the day of the competition, we will give you a link to the competition. When the time is over, we will look at the leaderboards to see who won the competition. Make sure that your name on hackerrank is your legal name (as seen on school documents), so that the extra credit can be handled easily.

Upcoming Competitions

The closest upcoming competition will occur at 4/15/2016 at Amador Valley High School. For this competition, make sure you have already created your hackerrank account (with your legal name, for extra credit purposes). To sign up, go to this link.
